Eureka! This could work out for my concept:

 

The key to learning how to remember names is to follow 5 step process by Ron White  <Ron's Page>

 

1. Focus your brain. When walking towards the person ask yourself, 'What is their name?'

 

2. Select an outstanding feature on their face
(big eyes, beard, scar, etc)

3. Create a picture for their name
Brian = brain, Lisa = Mona Lisa, Ron = Run,
Abby = a bee, Claudia= cloud …
This is a big step in learning how to remember names!
get use of a downloadable text file for the most typical names <Ron's File>

4. Imagine the picture for their name on the outstanding feature of their face with action and emotion


5. Review, review, review.
Ask yourself, 'Who did I meet today?

claudia (43)

Claudia teaches Informatics in a U_University to 1st and 2nd year students.

She needs to remember students and their names fast. Especially, it is relevant during seminar work, where they ask for individual feedback.

She loves her work and enjoys to be surrounded by young and energetic people.

She likes to train her brain with different mental training exercises.

basic structure of nameBook

This year the U_University offered a new application  nameBook to help the staff to speed-up memorizing the names and faces of the students.

The application concept is based on the scientifically proven key activities for memorizing the names:

 • Meet, greet and repeat

 • Create an association

 • Pick out a unique trait

 • Associate the face with an illogical visual cue

View on the students of 1A-class

The nameBook uses already available data base of students with their names and pictures.

 

This data base is fed by an online students portal, where all enrolled students can be found with their full names, their pictures and registered seminars and lectures.

Glossary includes visuals for each name translation

On the other hand the application uses a visual glossary of most typical names.

 

The glossary is inspired by Ron White

and available in a text form for download.

The concept of “translation” bases on creation of possible associative images for the sounding name e.g. the name Abby equals a bee etc.
